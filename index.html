Have a Life For Free

Being broke doesn't mean being bored. Here's how to actually enjoy yourself without spending.

  • 🌳
    Walks, parks, nature Every town has green space. It's free. It's good for you. It costs nothing and genuinely beats doom-scrolling.
  • 🧺
    Picnics Aldi food + a blanket + a park = a better afternoon than most pub sessions. £5 for two people. Bring a speaker.
  • 📚
    Libraries Free books, free WiFi, free warmth, free events. Audiobooks via the Libby app — free with your library card. Every single month.
  • 🎬
    Free streaming BBC iPlayer, ITVX, Channel 4, Pluto TV, Tubi — hundreds of hours of free content. You don't need Netflix AND Disney AND Prime.
  • 🏛️
    Free museums & galleries Most national museums in the UK are free. London especially — Natural History, V&A, Science Museum, National Gallery. All free.
  • 🎟️
    Free local events Search Eventbrite for free events near you — markets, gigs, festivals, outdoor cinema. Usually something on every weekend.

Budget That Doesn't Ruin Your Life

You don't need a spreadsheet. You need one rule and a bit of patience.

Most people aren't broke because they earn too little. They're broke because they spend it all the second it lands. The money was there. It just left immediately.

— The real reason
❌ Broke Loop
£0

Paid Friday. Spent by Sunday. Begging by Wednesday.

✅ Smart Loop
£200

Paid Friday. Moved £200 to savings immediately. Never saw it. Still there.

  • 💸
    Pay yourself first The second you get paid — move a set amount to savings. Even £20. Before you buy anything. You will not miss what you never see.
  • 📱
    Use a Plum pot Plum automatically sets money aside for you — rent, food, fun. When the fun pot is gone, fun is done. No willpower needed. Tom uses it. Get Plum free →
  • 📋
    Know your three numbers Monthly income. Monthly fixed costs (rent, bills, phone). What's left. That's your real budget. Most people don't know their own numbers.
  • 🔪
    Cut the subscriptions Check your bank statement right now. Count the subscriptions. Netflix, Spotify, Disney+, gym you don't use, apps you forgot. Cancel two today.
  • ⚡
    Switch energy tariff every year Use USwitch or Compare the Market. Takes 20 minutes. Saves £100-300 a year. Most people never do it and just pay more forever.
  • 📞
    Ring and threaten to cancel Sky, broadband, mobile — ring retention and say you're leaving. They will offer you a better deal 80% of the time. Every time. Takes 10 minutes.

The 3am Problem

It's not the £10. It's that you had nobody to call.

Tom lost his bus pass at 16. Called his dad reverse charges. Dad was pissed off. Came anyway. That's the whole solution. The question is never "how do I get home" — it's "do I have someone I can call?"

— The thing nobody teaches you
Why people end up begging on Reddit at 3am:
Not stupidity. Loneliness. No mate with a car. No dad to reverse charge. No manager they'd ask. Reddit answers when real people don't. It's a loneliness problem dressed up as a money problem.
📞

Who to Call — In Order

Before Reddit. Before anything. Run down this list first:

  • 1. Your manager — tonight, they can sort it
  • 2. Any mate with a car — even one you haven't spoken to in months
  • 3. Mum. Dad. Sibling. Awkward is fine. Stranded isn't.
  • 4. Ask the venue — pubs often sort staff getting home
  • 5. Google the night bus. It probably exists.
  • 6. Walk if under 30 mins and it's safe
  • 7. Sit in 24hr McDonald's til morning
  • 47. Reddit
💷

The £20 Rule

Before you go anywhere — night out, work, anywhere — you have £20 you will not touch. Emergency only. Bus fare. Taxi if it goes wrong.

Separate pocket. Separate card. Plum pot called GET HOME. Whatever stops you spending it. It just exists before you leave the house.

The rule:
No £20 set aside = you can't afford to go out yet. Wait one more week.
🤝

Be the Mate First

The person who picks you up at 3am already exists in your life. You just haven't maintained it. You only call them when you need something.

Give people lifts when you can. Lend a tenner when you have it. Show up. Be worth calling — then people answer.

💡 Tom's rule: Always offer the lift home first. Costs you nothing. Means everything later.

If you genuinely have nobody to call at 3am — that's worth noticing. Not judging. Just noticing. Because fixing the loneliness fixes most of the money problems too. People with real networks don't end up begging online. They get helped by the people around them before it ever gets that bad.
